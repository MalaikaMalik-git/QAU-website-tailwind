Introduction

The BS Computer Science program at QAU was started in 2013. It provides a well-integrated balance of theoretical and practical knowledge required for developing reliable and usable software tools for different fields. The program enables students to design, implement and manage systems combining scientific, computational, communication and multimedia facilities in different domains. The BS program is accredited by National Computing Education Accreditation Council.

BS Program Structure

During the B.S. students will be informed through research expertise of the faculty about the current and future issues affecting the usage and development of computer-based systems. Initially students learn basics of computing discipline through foundation courses and will build their knowledge of the discipline through core courses. In the last four semesters students will extend their knowledge in a focus area of their choice through elective courses. The final year compulsory project will give students the opportunity to consolidate and demonstrate the knowledge and skills gained from the entire course material.

Focus Areas

  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Data Science
  • Software Engineering
  • Human Centered Computing
  • Information Systems
  • Networks and Security

Courses Overview

The BS program consists of eight semesters spread across four years. Each semester has 15 to 18 credit hours. A total of 130 credit hours must be completed. The courses are categorized as follows:

  • Computing Core: Core computing concepts including AI, Data Science, Software Engineering, etc.
  • Domain Core: Theoretical foundations of CS.
  • Domain Elective: Specialization courses offered in last four semesters like AI, Deep Learning, Web Dev, etc.
  • Maths and Supporting: Mathematical and statistical fundamentals.
  • Elective Supporting: Social and management sciences.
  • General Education: Non-CS subjects like English, Mathematics, Physics, Ethics, etc.
